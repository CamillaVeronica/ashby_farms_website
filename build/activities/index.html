The surrounding area of Kent and East Sussex offers something for everyone with beaches and beautiful countryside to explore, historic towns such as Rye and Canterbury and a great food and drink culture with some of the top vineyards in the country on your doorstep.

You can find more ideas of things to do and see what we are getting up to on Facebook, Instagram, Twitter and our blog. The Visit Ashford and Tenterden website also offers a good list of what's on in the area.

Get Outdoors

  • Beaches

    Explore the dunes of Camber Sands and Dungeness, England's only desert

  • Cycling

    Hop on your bike to explore the area. Why not try one of these routes?

  • Hiking

    The marsh and downs are fantastic for hikers and walkers

  • Fishing

    In our lakes

  • Shooting

    On our farmland

Family

  • Camber Sands

    These sandy beaches are a perfect spot to enjoy good weather and practice your sandcastle building.

  • Port Lymne

    Lions, tigers, elephants and more... Head here for a safari experience without leaving our shores.

  • Miniature Steam Train

    The minature steam train to Romney, Hythe and Dymchurch is a slice of history with a novel twist.

  • Rare Breeds Centre

    Meet unusual breeds of all your favourite farm animals.

Foodie

  • Vineyards

    The rock of the champagne region runs through Kent and East Sussex allowing us to produce some excellent wines. See for yourself at vineyards such as Gusbourne and Chapel Down.

  • Breweries

    Kent is famous for its hops, why not try some of our real ale at these local breweries.

  • Kentish Fruit

    This isn't called the Garden of England for nothing. In the summer there are lots of farms that you can visit to pick your own.

  • Whitstable Oysters

    Just 45 minutes away Whitstable offers some of the finest seafood in the country. It even has a festival devoted to oysters every July.

  • Rye Scallops

    Rye is very easy to get to and is another seafood-lover's paradise. It has a scallop festival in February every year.

Culture and History

  • Canterbury

    Famed for its Cathedral and as a destination for pilgrimage, most famously in The Canterbury Tales this is a fantastic city to visit on your trip

  • Smugglers Caves

    Learn about the role of Romney Marsh and the Kent and Sussex coast in smuggling at the Smugglers Caves in Hastings.

  • Sissinghurst

    This property has a rich and varied history, not to mention beautiful gardens to explore.

  • Dover Castle

    From Tudor times through to the tunnels used during WW2 this castle, known as 'The Key to England', has something to interest any history buff.
